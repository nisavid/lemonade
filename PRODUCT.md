# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Lemonade serves local AI users, developers, app embedders, AMD AI PC owners, and contributors.

Local AI users want private multimodal AI on their own machines without needing to understand model formats, backend engines, or hardware-specific setup details up front. Developers want familiar local APIs for existing tools and experiments. App builders and embedders want Lemonade to become an invisible runtime inside their own experiences. AMD AI PC and Radeon users want their hardware to do useful local AI work. Contributors extend model, engine, platform, and hardware coverage.

## Product Purpose

Lemonade makes useful local multimodal AI feel as reachable and standard as cloud AI. It hides backend and hardware complexity behind a local service, visible model manager, familiar APIs, and optional embeddable runtime.

Success means a user can install Lemonade, run or connect something valuable in minutes, and still see enough model, backend, server, and hardware state to trust what is happening and recover when something needs attention.

## Brand Personality

Lemonade should feel refreshing, pragmatic, and enabling.

It can be friendly and a little playful at the threshold, especially in public brand and onboarding surfaces. Product surfaces should stay calm, exact, and trustworthy: clear state, clear actions, and enough personality to feel approachable without becoming toy-like.

## Anti-References

Lemonade should not feel like a cloud AI upsell, a vendor-locked hardware demo appliance, a GUI-only chat toy, a single-backend runner, an expert-only config maze, an enterprise control plane first, or a broad AI workspace that tries to replace Open WebUI, AnythingLLM, GAIA, or partner apps.

Avoid patterns that make Lemonade seem like the destination for every AI workflow. The product should make local AI understandable, useful, connectable, and embeddable.

## Design Principles

- Foundation, not destination.
- Happy path before expert depth.
- Standards over invention.
- Hardware-aware without hardware burden.
- Visibility proves capability; automation removes friction.
- Community-open, AMD-optimized.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Focus on product accessibility fundamentals without presenting a formal compliance claim: strong contrast in dark and light themes, keyboard access, non-color-only status, reduced motion where relevant, visible focus, readable command and code surfaces, clear download and hardware-state explanations, and recovery paths that treat user confusion as product friction.
